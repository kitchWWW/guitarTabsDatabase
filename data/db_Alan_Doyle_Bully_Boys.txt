Alan plays nearly all chords in this song with the 3rd and 4th fingers anchored, except in the bridge.
He uses a variety of D chords with different bass notes (C/G/F#) that vary across performances. Use them
as you like or just use the Dsus4 everywhere.

Here are the major varitions he uses.

G       (G6): 320000 (played in the bridge on the lines that lead with Am)[/tab]
[ch]C[/ch]    ([ch]Csus2[/ch]): x30033
[ch]Em[/ch]     ([ch]Em7[/ch]): 020033
[tab][ch]D[/ch]    ([ch]Dsus4[/ch]): xx0233 (most common [ch]D[/ch])
D (Dsus4/F#): 2x0233 (used in the first and third lines in the bridge or can be used everywhere)[/tab]
D  (Dsus4/E): xx2233 (used in "farewell to France")
D  (Dsus4/C): x3x233 (also used in "farewell to France" in some performances)

At the end of each chorus, Alan does a walkdown on the final "row" as follows:
x3x033
x2x033
x00033
3x0033

The first verse is sung a cappella on the record and in most recordings until the walkdown on the last G.

the Flat Duo Jets - "Because I Love You" - Track 13 of "Red Tango," 1996.

Tuned lower than Standard Tuning, but not by a whole note. Tune in 432 Hz to E Standard.
This gets you close, but maybe not 100% there.

Lyrics are beneath the tab in quotation marks, "like this." Also,
notes to individual sections of the tab are in-between brackets,
(like this.)

Note before beginning - if you struggle to play some of the parts for this one in the Choruses,
perhaps you're using a guitar with a neck width that's too wide. The guitar Dexter used for 
this song was likely his Silvertone 1448, which has a neck nut width of 1.650", and so you may 
do better to use a different guitar for this song.

[Gear / Equipment Likely Used for This Song:]
=============================================

Dexter Romweber tended to use a (Danelectro Built) Silvertone 1448 Guitar for 
much of the Flat Duo Jets' history. He didn't always, he also had a 
Danelectro U1 or U2, but the 1448 is a good choice for this song
(and also for much of the Flat Duo Jets' discography.)

The 1448 is a short-scale guitar with a 23.50" scale, a nut width of 1.650"
(which is only just a bit wider than a Fender's 1 5/8" width,) and it has
one 1950s - 1960s spec Danelectro "Lipstick Tube" Pickup, and a body that's 
usually made of a Pine Frame with a Masonite Top and Back glued on. 
The Neck is Poplar, with a Brazilian Rosewood Fretboard. 

These models are still cheap (relatively) 
as of late 2023, going in the range of $200 - $500, depending on condition
and if you also get the Case that originally came with it,
which has an Amp included inside it.

If you get one with the Amp in Case, I advise *against* playing the amp,
unless it's been modified for safety. The old design can shock you 
through your strings, though its tone does sound fun.

I don't know which guitar amplifier, or which settings, were used for the 
recording of "Because I Love You" though.

This is my take at this wonderful song. I absolutely adore this version of it 
(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_K8yHfU6bSw) and took this and not the studio version as basis for my tab. 
He plays it capo 5 so I am guessing (hoping) he has his guitar tuned one step down. Maybe not, maybe he 
used a different tuning alltogether.

Tuning: (4th) D-String half tone down to C#
Capo:   3 (all chords relative to capo 3)

Chords can then be played with the open 4th string C# (unless stated otherwise, mainly the E chord needs a 
3 on that string, A and B are optional, see Outro) and B and e strings. Or you make it a quiet acoustic 
version and keep picking.

Instrumental Part:

C#m7        B       A          B          A   G#m
C#m7        B       A          B           

Verse 3 (Ich bin viel zu träge...) same as Verses 1&amp;2

Chorus 2 same as Chorus 1, except I have the impression they (maybe only the bass) quickly go back to G#m on "Und wenns's am" 
between the C#m7 and the A (schönsten ist...). The same goes for the next line, between C#m7
and A on "dann nehm ich", then go back to A for "Reißaus...".

The picking pattern comes back for Verse number 4 (Jetzt steht meine Welt still…).

Chorus 3: Same as the first two but let it fade out on a whole bar of A (instead of A B C#m7 A) at the end 
of it (Glaub’s mir das ist es nicht wert).

Then you can change back to a mix of chords and strums again for the epic piano outro that I tried to get 
close to on the guitar. Sort out how you want to play it yourself, I added some melody/slides/chord 
variations to imitate the piano.

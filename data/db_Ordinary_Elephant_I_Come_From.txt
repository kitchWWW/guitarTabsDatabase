Pretty simple song, though the strum pattern might be tricky to pick up on and maintain.
The difficult part comes from playing it with feeling and finding the unique lyrical
arrangement. The entire song is three chords, except for one Em in the Bridge. Variants of G with hammer-ons
the A string, D, and C with hammer-ons the D string. I find the hammer-ons to be optional, they do add a
little variation in sound. Super fun to play and usually a crowd pleaser. Also, my first tab, so go easy!
